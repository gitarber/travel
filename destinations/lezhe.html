About Lezha

Lezha, an ancient city with over 2000 years of history, stands as a testament to Albania's rich cultural heritage. Once known as Lissus in ancient times, it was an important Illyrian settlement and later became famous as the burial place of Albania's national hero, Skanderbeg.

Today, the city combines its historical significance with modern coastal charm, offering visitors a unique blend of cultural sites, traditional cuisine, and nearby beaches along the Adriatic coast.
