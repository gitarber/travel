About Gjirokastër

Gjirokastër, known as the "City of Stone," is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of Albania's most remarkable historic towns. Perched on the slopes of the Gjerë mountains, its old town is characterized by its distinctive Ottoman architecture and impressive castle overlooking the valley.

The city's unique architecture features tall stone houses with slate roofs, wooden balconies, and whitewashed stone walls, earning it the nickname "City of a Thousand Steps."
